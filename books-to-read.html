Recommended Reading

Here you'll find the three books I recommend the most to anyone who wants to try creative writing

What you won't find here is some get rich quick huckster bull-shit about how if you just plot your story this way or that, you'll sell a million copies easy.
Just the good stuff.

On Writing- Stephen King

Stephen King's on writing is part autobiography and part a love letter to the craft.

He won't give you incredibly specific advice, but the advice he gives is the only good advice that's out there:

  • Read a lot
  • Write a lot
  • Give yourself a place to work that doesn't put art before life
  • Show up everyday

Elements of Style- Strunk and Weiss

You might know the Weiss half from this duo: he's the guy who wrote perennial elementary school favorite Charlotte's Web.

What you might not know is that this style guide is assigned around the the USA as one of the textbooks for freshman composition.

Not sure when to use a semicolon? Whether you should have a comma there? This book will tell you!

Poetic Meter and Poetic Form- Paul Fussell

If you ever wanted to pick up on the more technical aspects of poetry, Fussell's book and a good anthology is all you'll need.

Even if you don't plan on writing any poetry yourself, readint his book could give you some great insight into how to include rhythm in your prose.

Remember that some of the greatest novels ever written were really just prose poems!
